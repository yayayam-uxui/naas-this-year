## What the brief actually asks (reference — from `Final_Project_Brief_Review.pdf`, p.4–6)

The Stretch Zone = **hire two roles we never staffed in class**. It is a metaphor for two artifacts, not two headcount:

- **Role 1 — fixed for everyone: New-Hire Onboarding Plan.** A complete, end-to-end onboarding package for a first teammate: who the company is, the customer, the product, brand voice + rules, tools, dos and don'ts, what good work looks like.
- **Role 2 — pick at least one from a menu of four:**
  - **A · Product Marketing / Go-To-Market** — positioning line, messaging house, target segment + buying moment, channels, week-one launch plan.
  - **B · Business Model & Operating Plan** — revenue model, who pays for what, cost drivers, 3–5 load-bearing assumptions, stress-tested.
  - **C · Customer Success Playbook** — onboard / support / retain + at least one *real, finished* self-serve asset (FAQ or support reply).
  - **D · First Hire — Job Description & First 90 Days** — which role you'd hire first and why (biggest gap), a real JD, interview questions, a 30/60/90 plan.

Graded on **judgment, not perfection**: "a thoughtful first attempt at a role you'd never done beats a polished copy." Feeds "Thinking outside the box" (25 pts).
