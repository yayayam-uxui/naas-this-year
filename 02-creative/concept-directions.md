## Direction A — "The 6:30 AM Call" ⭐ recommended
**Genre:** Cinematic mini-story. One protagonist, one arc.
**Mood:** Premium, warm-but-punchy. Nike-morning energy: dark blue pre-dawn → golden momentum. Sound-first — the ad is *carried by audio*, like a great Pepsi/Nike spot.

**Story:**
5:59 AM, dark bedroom. A phone doesn't *ping* — it **RINGS**. She answers, half asleep.
We hear the call: *"Hey champ. Your time to shine is right now."* (Ego Pumper)
Cut to a montage of one week in her life, each beat triggered by a different persona calling at the exact right moment:
- **Pragmatist** at 6:30 — she's lacing running shoes.
- **Tough Love** at 14:00 — she closes Instagram, opens the deck she's been avoiding.
- **Supportive Partner** at 22:30 — she almost skips studying; doesn't.
Final beat: she's about to give up on something big. Phone rings. She looks at it. Smiles. Answers.
**"We've got your back."** Logo.

**The craft trick:** the ringtone becomes the rhythmic backbone of the soundtrack — every ring lands on the beat. That's the "premium" tell.

**Why it wins:** the product is *sound* — and this ad demos it literally. All 4 personas appear (the differentiator), one clear thing (a call at the right moment changes the moment), emotional arc.
**Risk:** one protagonist across ~7 shots = character-consistency challenge (mitigation: Seedance 2.0 reference identity / Midjourney keyframes).

## Direction B — "With Us vs. Without Us" (split screen)
**Genre:** Split-screen comparison, rhythmic editing.
**Mood:** Graphic, clean, high-contrast. Apple-ad minimalism with a wink.

**Story:** Same person, same week, two timelines. Left: push notification arrives → swiped away → snooze, doomscroll, gym bag gathering dust. Right: the phone RINGS → she answers → moves. The sides drift further apart until the final frame: left-her watches right-her cross a finish line. One line of copy: **"Same you. One phone call apart."**

**Why it wins:** the clearest possible articulation of the value prop; maps 1:1 to Higgsfield's **Before and After** preset → fastest to produce; it was Yam's own instinct on the team call.
**Risk:** familiar format — reads more "performance ad" than "Pepsi brand film." Split-screen doubles the shots to generate.

## Direction C — "The Nag Orchestra" (humor-forward)
**Genre:** Comedic escalation, ensemble cast.
**Mood:** Bold, cheeky, Super-Bowl-spot energy. Big music, hard cuts.

**Story:** Four strangers, four moments of weakness, four very different calls:
donut frozen mid-air (*"Hey. Put it down. You know why I'm calling."* — Tough Love), a terrifying email + hype call (Ego Pumper), a 6:30 gym window narrated like a weather report (Pragmatist), a almost-cancelled evening run (Supportive Partner).
Punchline: **"They all chose their own caller. Because deep down, you know which voice you need."**

**Why it wins:** funniest and most shareable; the 4 personas ARE the structure; ensemble cast = every shot is a *new* person → zero character-consistency problem (production-friendly!).
**Risk:** comedy timing is the hardest thing to direct in AI video; humor that misses feels cheap — the opposite of the goal.

---

## Production insight that de-risks ALL directions 🔑
The personas' voices come **through a phone** — meaning **no lip-sync needed** for the AI voices. The on-screen humans barely speak. Voice is generated separately (high quality, full control over the 4 personas) and laid over the visuals in the edit. This kills the single biggest AI-video failure mode (bad lip-sync) *by concept design*.
