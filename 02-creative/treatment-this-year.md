# Treatment — "This Year" (The 6:30 AM Call × New Year's Resolutions)
_Direction A chosen by Yam (14/7), merged with her New-Year's-resolutions framing. Drafted 15 July 2026. Status: awaiting Yam's approval._

## The one clear thing
**A goal with a voice behind it gets done.** Everything in the film serves this sentence.

## Logline
Every January she writes the same resolutions. This year one thing changed: her phone doesn't ping — it *rings*.

## Format
60 seconds · cinematic photoreal · one protagonist (woman, mid-20s) · 16:9 (9:16 recut later for social)
**Sound-first design:** the ringtone is a musical motif — every ring lands on the beat. Persona voices are *phone audio* (band-passed, from the device) → **zero lip-sync needed on screen.**

## Visual grammar
Midnight blue + firework gold (NYE) → cold pre-dawn blue (the turn) → warmer, golder light as momentum builds → full gold at the final NYE. Color IS the arc.

---

## The narrator layer (added 15/7 — Yam's idea, developed in ping-pong)
A "Morgan Freeman-style" voiceover: deep, warm gravel, unhurried, wry. **Original generated voice only — no cloning of any real person's voice** (legal + academic-integrity line we do not cross; logged).

Two variants on the table:
- **V1 · Classic omniscient** — narrator frames open + close, calls own the middle. Safe, anthemic.
- **V2 · The narrator IS the EA (recommended)** — the app's devoted assistant proudly narrates its boss's year ("The user is the Boss" ethos, personified):
  - Open: *"That's my boss. Every January, she makes herself three promises."* / *"And every February — she stops picking up."*
  - Turn: *"So this year… I started calling."* → RING.
  - Close: *"Eleven months. Four hundred calls. Zero excuses."* → her phone rings, she smiles → narrator: *"We've got your back."*
  - The reveal: the voice narrating the film is the voice on her phone. Narration = product demo.
- Discipline: narrator ≤ 45 words total; persona calls keep the middle (20–45s) untouched.

## The film (8 shots)

**SHOT 1 · Hook — Midnight (0–6s)**
NYE. Fireworks through an apartment window. Close on her phone: she types resolutions — *Run a 10K. Finish the portfolio. Save $5K.* Glasses clink somewhere. Supertitle: **Jan 1. 00:03.**

**SHOT 2 · Problem — The graveyard (6–14s)**
Time-lapse of the year dying quietly: running shoes still in the box; lockscreen stacking pushes — *"Reminder: Gym 💪"* — swiped away, again, again, again. An offscreen friend: *"So… how's the 10K going?"* She laughs it off. Everyone knows this laugh.

**SHOT 3 · The turn (14–20s)**
Hard cut to black. Supertitle: **This year is different.**
5:59 AM, dark bedroom. The phone doesn't ping. It **RINGS**. She squints. Answers.

**SHOTS 4–7 · The calls (20–45s) — four personas, four moments, ringtone on the beat**
- **Ego Pumper · 6:30 AM:** *"Hey champ. That 10K won't run itself. Time to shine — let's go."* → she laces the shoes. Pre-dawn blue.
- **Pragmatist · 13:00:** *"It's 1 PM. Portfolio window: ninety minutes. Right now."* → Instagram closes, laptop opens.
- **Tough Love · 21:00:** couch, snack mid-air. *"You said this mattered. Put it down."* → smirk. Puts it down.
- **Supportive Partner · 22:30:** desk lamp, tired eyes. *"I know you're tired. Ten more minutes — I've got you."* → she keeps going.

**SHOT 8 · Payoff — NYE, one year later (45–55s)**
Fireworks again. Same couch, same friend. She opens last year's list: ✓ ✓ ✓. Friend: *"Okay — HOW?"*
Her phone rings softly in her hand. She smiles, standing to answer: *"Someone's got my back."*

**END CARD (55–60s)**
Logo + name (⚠️ waiting on Ido) · **"We've got your back."** · *Choose your voice* — 4 persona glyphs.

## Why this works
- **Brand-voice native:** every call line is built from the team's approved voice doc — "Right now", "You said this mattered", "Let's go", "I've got you". Video ↔ strategy consistency (10 rubric points).
- **The product demos itself:** the whole ad is literally the product experience — a call at the right moment.
- **All 4 personas shown** = the differentiator, in 25 seconds, without a features list.
- **Universal hook:** NYE resolutions is Yam's original instinct — the most relatable failure ritual on earth.
- **Production-safe:** phone voices = no lip-sync; 8 shots = within the 970-credit budget with keyframe discipline.

## Production plan (per verified research)
1. **Script punch-up** — writers-room agents pitch alt lines per call (Kalshi method), Yam picks.
2. **Keyframes first** — 8 opening/closing stills (Midjourney / Imagen / Higgsfield Soul), protagonist locked from one reference set. Iterate HERE (cheap), not in video.
3. **Test free:** Google Vids' 10 free Veo generations + Higgsfield fast modes for 1–2 pilot shots.
4. **Animate** locked frames via first/last-frame (Flow/Veo 3.1, Kling, Seedance 2.0 — pilot decides).
5. **Voices** — 4 persona voice lines as phone-filtered audio (Gemini/Higgsfield audio/ElevenLabs), mixed in edit.
6. **Assemble** — edit, ringtone-on-beat music, grade, end card slot for Ido's logo.

## Dependencies / risks
- **Name + logo (Ido):** end card and the spoken final line stay name-free until locked ("Someone's got my back" — deliberately works without a brand name).
- **Timeline:** submission Thursday 16/7 → shots must be locked tonight, generation + edit tomorrow.
- Character drift across shots → mitigation: same reference stills fed to every generation; wardrobe/hair kept simple and distinctive.
