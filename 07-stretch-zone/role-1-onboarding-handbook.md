## Layer 1 · General — for anyone who joins WISP

### Word from the founder
Welcome. I will keep this short, because WISP is a company that respects your time the way we respect our users'.

We built WISP because the world got very good at reminding people and very bad at moving them. Notifications pile up. Nobody acts. We decided on the opposite: one real voice, at the right moment, saying the thing a sticky note never could. *"You said this mattered. Right now. I've got you."*

You are our first teammate. That is not a small thing. It means the company outgrew one person, and you are the part we could not do alone. Make it yours.

### Who we are, and why we exist
**WISP replaces the ignorable push notification with a live voice call. We don't remind you. We move you.** The user is the boss. We are the executive assistant who eliminates their excuses: devoted, decisive, impossible to swipe away.

### Who our customer is
- **The Unbossed Hustler** — freelancers and solo founders whose isolation is quietly killing their output. No manager, no standup, no one to answer to. We become the one who checks in.
- **The Procrastinating Student** — the Crisis Crammer and the Repeat Quitter, met with the right persona instead of another silent miss.

### The product
- **Four personas, chosen at onboarding:** Tough Love · The Pragmatist · Supportive Partner · Ego Pumper. Same mission, four different ways of getting you there.
- **The loop:** Set Goal → AI Call → Take Action → Report.
- **The promise:** an original AI voice. We never clone a real person.

### Brand voice — the non-negotiables
- **Always:** "Right now." · "I've got you." · "You said this mattered." · "Let's go."
- **Never:** "Just a friendly reminder…" · "Whenever you get a chance…" · "Sorry to bother you…"
- **Tone:** decisive, devoted, uncompromising. Active voice, present tense. One clear line beats three soft ones.

### How we work
**We are AI-native, and AI-reflexive.** When you hit a problem, your first instinct here is to reach for AI, fast. Not as a gimmick, as a reflex. We build the product with AI, we run the company with AI, and yes, we onboard *you* with AI. A tool is a professional choice, not a religion, but the default move is: try the AI path first.

**We are coffee-forward.** The best ideas here do not happen in meetings, they happen over coffee. So it is a real value, not a perk: in your first days you will book a **30-minute coffee with a teammate**, and then keep booking them. Collaboration at WISP starts with a cup and a real conversation. Book as many as you can. It is the fastest way to learn who we are, what we do, and who to reach out to.

### Tools we use, and how
Claude Code and Cowork (orchestration and skills) · Higgsfield and ElevenLabs (video and voice) · Gamma (decks) · ffmpeg and ImageMagick (edit and compositing) · Perplexity and Gemini (research). Reach for the one that fits the task, and reach fast.

### What good work looks like here
- Show the process, not just the result. We keep the dead ends.
- Verify before you ship. A fabricated fact is never "the AI's fault," it is ours.
- One company, one look, in every asset.

### Your first days
You will not shadow a slide deck. You will live in the product.
1. **WISP calls you.** Day one, you get your acceptance call (press play above). Take the walkthrough it gives you.
2. **Dogfood WISP, for real.** Set a genuine personal goal and run it through the product for at least **10 hours** across your first weeks. Pick a persona. Feel all four. And, on purpose, **miss one goal**, so you feel exactly what our user feels when WISP catches them. You cannot build for a feeling you have never had.
3. **Listen to real user calls.** Before you touch anything, hear how people actually use us. Read the raw goals they set.
4. **Book the coffees.** Thirty minutes each, as many as you can, across the team.

Whatever else you need, you ask and we get it for you. That is the deal.

## Layer 2 · Tailored — because you are our Growth Engineer

*(The handbook above is for anyone who joins WISP. This layer is for the specific role we hired first: you. Full detail lives in your First-Hire package, Role 2.)*

You are not just any first teammate. You are the person we decided WISP most needs: a **Growth Engineer** — the consumer-side cousin of the emerging "GTM Engineer" role. Why you, and why first, is the whole story of your hire package.

**The one thing you own: activation.** Specifically, getting a new user to the moment that predicts everything — **they complete their first AI call and act on it.** Not a vanity metric. It is our core loop, and it is yours.

**You inherit the product, but you are not stuck with it.** If moving activation means changing the onboarding, the first call, or the loop itself, that is yours to ship. In a product-led company the product *is* the growth channel, so the growth hire has real product surface, not just the funnel around it.

So your dogfooding above is not only culture, it is the job. When you run your own goal through WISP and feel that first call land, you are standing exactly where every user stands at their most important moment. The metric you will spend 90 days moving is a feeling you will have had in week one.

Your ramp flows straight into your **30/60/90 plan** (in the First-Hire package): Days 0–30 you instrument the funnel and validate who actually activates; 31–60 you automate the lifecycle nudges; 61–90 you stand up the experiment loop. Onboarding tells you *how to come up to speed.* The 30/60/90 tells you *what to deliver.*

Welcome aboard. Let's go.
