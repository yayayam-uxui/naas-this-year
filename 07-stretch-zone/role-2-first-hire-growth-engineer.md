# WISP · First Hire — Growth Engineer
### Stretch Zone · Role 2 (First Hire — Job Description & First 90 Days)

## Why this role, and why first

WISP is a one-person company. Deciding the first hire is really deciding the one thing the founder cannot do alone. It is not "more marketing" — the content and the brand already exist. It is turning WISP's own AI into a repeatable engine that gets strangers to pick up, trust, and act on their first voice call. That is a systems problem, and it is the difference between a clever demo and a company.

**The role, and its lineage.** In 2023 the "GTM Engineer" appeared — coined by Clay — when teams realized one technical builder armed with AI and no-code tooling could do the work of a whole revenue-operations team. Demand then exploded: roughly **+205% year over year** into 2025, about 100 new listings a month. But the canonical GTM Engineer is a **B2B outbound** role (enrich leads, score accounts, sequence email). WISP is **B2C / prosumer** and product-led, so we hire its consumer cousin: the **Growth Engineer**. Same DNA — automation, data, experimentation — pointed at a consumer funnel instead of a pipeline. Choosing the right adaptation of a brand-new role, rather than a title we saw in class, is the judgment call.

**Why first, not fifth.** An AI-native product that instruments and automates its funnel from day one never has to untangle the "frankenstack" of disconnected tools that established companies spend years fixing. And the economics are the whole point of a one-person unicorn: as Norwest puts it, *"before you hire five SDRs, ask whether one GTM Engineer could rebuild the machine they are about to operate."* One Growth Engineer plus WISP's own AI beats a team of five, at a fraction of the cost.

## The mandate (one line)
**Own activation.** Get a new user to complete their first AI call and act on it, fast, and keep them. Inherit the product, and change it wherever that moves activation.

## Job Description (real, postable)

**Growth Engineer — WISP** · remote-friendly · first hire · reports to the founder

WISP replaces the ignorable push notification with a live AI voice call. We don't remind you, we move you. We are looking for our first teammate: a builder who turns our own AI into a growth engine.

**What you'll own**
- Instrument the full funnel (signup → activation → retention → referral) and make it measurable.
- Define and move our **activation milestone**: a user completes their first AI call and acts on it.
- Build onboarding and lifecycle automation across push, SMS, email, and in-app.
- Ship product changes to the activation surface. You inherit the product, and you can change it.
- Run the experiment loop: hypothesize, test, measure, scale what wins, kill what doesn't.
- Stand up WISP's first referral / PLG loop.

**Must-have**
- SQL, and comfort inside a product-analytics / CRM data model.
- One automation tool (n8n, Make, Zapier, Hightouch, or similar).
- LLM / prompting fluency. You reach for AI first, by reflex.
- A commercial mindset: you know which changes actually move the metric.
- A tinkerer's instinct. We care that you ship and learn, not where you studied. Formal engineering training is not required.

**Nice-to-have**
- Python; a CDP + analytics stack (Segment, Amplitude, PostHog); lifecycle tooling (Braze, Customer.io); reverse-ETL; vibe-coding tools (Lovable, Bolt) for quick internal apps.

**How we work**
- AI-native and AI-reflexive. Coffee-forward. Show the process, verify before you ship, one company one look.

## How we screen and interview

**Screen for evidence, not credentials.** We want a portfolio of things you shipped: an automation, an experiment, a scrappy internal tool. A candidate who rebuilt a funnel over a weekend beats a resume full of titles.

**The questions we would actually ask**
1. **Systems.** "Walk me through an automation or data pipeline you built end to end. What broke, and how did you find out?"
2. **The live case.** "A WISP user signs up but never completes their first call. You have our data. What are the first three things you instrument, and what is your first experiment?"
3. **AI reflex.** "Show me the last thing you built where AI did the real work, not autocomplete. Where did it get things wrong, and how did you catch it?"
4. **Product judgment.** "You find that changing the first call's script lifts activation, but the founder loves the current script. How do you handle it?"
5. **Prioritization.** "Activation, retention, referral. With one month and one metric, which do you touch first for a voice app, and why?"

## The first 90 days (30 / 60 / 90)

**Days 0–30 · Come up to speed, then find the truth in the data.**
- Onboard the WISP way: dogfood the product (set a real goal, feel all four personas, miss one on purpose), listen to real user calls, book the coffees.
- Instrument the funnel (Segment + Amplitude or PostHog).
- Define WISP's activation milestone precisely, and validate **which persona actually activates and retains**, turning our directional ICP (freelancers, solo founders, students) from a marketing hypothesis into a data-backed model.

**Days 31–60 · Automate the moments that matter.**
- Build the onboarding and lifecycle nudge journeys (Braze or Customer.io) that carry a new user to their first call within 24 hours.
- Ship the first product changes that cut time-to-first-call.

**Days 61–90 · Turn it into an engine.**
- Stand up the experimentation loop (A/B) and report the first activation lift with numbers.
- Launch WISP's first referral / PLG loop.

Success at 90 days is not a launch. It is a measurable lift in the share of new users who complete and act on their first call, and a funnel that now runs as instrumented, testable systems.
